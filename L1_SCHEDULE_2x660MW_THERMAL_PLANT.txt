PROJECT DETAILS:
================================================================================
Project Type:           Greenfield Supercritical Coal Thermal Power Plant
Capacity:              2 Units × 660 MW = 1,320 MW (Total)
Location:              Nagpur, Maharashtra, India
Main Contractor:       Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (BHEL)
Technology:            Supercritical Pulverized Coal
Environmental Control: ESP, FGD (Flue Gas Desulfurization), SCR
Total Project Duration: 44 Months (3.67 Years from Project Start)
Unit-1 COD Target:     Month 37 (Approximately 37 months from start)
Unit-2 COD Target:     Month 41 (Approximately 41 months from start)

TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS:
================================================================================
Steam Parameters:
├─ Main Steam Pressure: 255 atm(a) / 25.8 MPa
├─ Main Steam Temperature: 568°C
└─ Reheat Steam Temperature: 596°C

Turbine Specifications:
├─ Type: 3000 RPM, Tandem Compound
├─ Configuration: Single Reheat, Regenerative, Condensing
├─ Design: 1 HP (High Pressure) + 1 IP (Intermediate Pressure) + 2 LP (Low Pressure) Cylinders
└─ Total Capacity: 660 MW per unit (2 units = 1,320 MW)

Generator Specifications:
├─ Type: 780 MVA per unit
├─ Total: 2 × 780 MVA = 1,560 MVA
└─ Voltage: 13.8 kV (generator terminal)

Coal Consumption:
├─ Rate: 7.18 MMTPA (Million Metric Tonnes Per Annum)
├─ At 85% Plant Load Factor (PLF)
└─ Source: Linked coal supply

Water Source:
├─ Treated Sewage Water: 25.2 MM³ per annum (from Nagpur city)
├─ Pench Dam Water: 10.8 MM³ per annum
└─ Total: 36 MM³ per annum

Emission Control:
├─ ESP (Electrostatic Precipitator): Fly ash collection
├─ FGD (Flue Gas Desulfurization): SO₂ removal
└─ SCR (Selective Catalytic Reduction): NOₓ removal
